## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create intuitive and effective user experiences by researching user needs, designing interfaces, and validating designs through testing. They work closely with Product Managers and Developers to ensure the product meets user expectations and usability standards.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and analyze user behavior patterns
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs
- Develop and maintain design systems and style guides
- Collaborate with developers to ensure design implementation fidelity
- Conduct usability testing and iterate on feedback
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design practices

### Goals
- Deliver user-centered, accessible experiences
- Reduce friction in user workflows
- Maintain consistency across product interfaces
- Validate design decisions through user research and data

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings and usability reports
- Design handoff documentation and specifications
- Weekly sync with Product and Engineering leads

### Typical Interactions
- **Developers**: Collaborate on design feasibility and implementation details
- **Product Managers**: Align on user needs, feature priorities, and success metrics
- **Data Analysts**: Review user behavior data to inform design decisions
- **Customer Support Liaison**: Gather insights on user pain points and friction areas

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts collect, process, and analyze data to inform product decisions and measure outcomes. They transform raw data into actionable insights that guide strategy, feature prioritization, and performance optimization.

### Responsibilities
- Design and implement data tracking and analytics infrastructure
- Create dashboards and reports for key performance metrics
- Conduct A/B tests and analyze experiment results
- Identify trends, patterns, and anomalies in product usage
- Collaborate with stakeholders to define success metrics
- Provide data-driven recommendations for product improvements

### Goals
- Enable data-driven decision making across the organization
- Ensure accurate and timely metric reporting
- Identify opportunities for product optimization
- Validate hypotheses through rigorous analysis

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metric reviews and trend analysis
- Experiment design and results presentations
- Dashboard documentation and data definitions
- Ad-hoc analysis requests and findings reports

### Typical Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Define success metrics and analyze feature performance
- **Developers**: Implement tracking and instrumentation requirements
- **UX Designers**: Share user behavior insights to inform design decisions
- **Project Managers**: Provide data for status reports and decision making

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers design, build, and maintain the infrastructure and automation that enables reliable, scalable software delivery. They bridge development and operations by implementing CI/CD pipelines, monitoring systems, and infrastructure as code.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment automation
- Manage cloud infrastructure and orchestration platforms
- Implement monitoring, logging, and alerting systems
- Ensure system reliability, scalability, and security
- Optimize deployment processes and reduce deployment friction
- Respond to incidents and conduct root cause analysis
- Maintain infrastructure documentation and runbooks

### Goals
- Minimize deployment risk and maximize deployment frequency
- Ensure system uptime and reliability
- Reduce manual toil through automation
- Enable fast feedback loops for developers

### Typical Communication
- Incident reports and postmortems
- Infrastructure change notifications
- Performance and reliability metrics
- On-call handoffs and runbook updates

### Typical Interactions
- **Developers**: Collaborate on build requirements, deployment strategies, and debugging production issues
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate release schedules and communicate deployment risks
- **Data Analysts**: Provide infrastructure for data pipelines and analytics systems
- **Customer Support Liaison**: Communicate service status and resolve customer-impacting incidents

## Customer Support Liaison

### Role Summary
Customer Support Liaisons serve as the bridge between customers and the product team, ensuring customer feedback informs product development while providing timely support and issue resolution. They advocate for customer needs and help prioritize improvements based on user impact.

### Responsibilities
- Triage and route customer issues and feature requests
- Document common problems and maintain knowledge base articles
- Escalate high-priority or complex issues to engineering
- Gather and synthesize customer feedback for product planning
- Communicate product updates and feature releases to customers
- Track customer satisfaction metrics and improvement trends

### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction and retention
- Reduce time to resolution for customer issues
- Surface actionable product insights from customer interactions
- Ensure clear communication between customers and product teams

### Typical Communication
- Daily ticket reviews and escalation updates
- Weekly summaries of customer feedback themes
- Release notes and customer communication drafts
- Escalation reports for critical customer issues

### Typical Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Share customer feedback to inform roadmap priorities
- **Developers**: Collaborate on bug reproduction, troubleshooting, and workarounds
- **UX Designers**: Provide real-world user feedback and usability issues
- **Project Managers**: Report on customer-impacting issues and feature requests

## Cross-Functional Collaboration

Effective project delivery requires coordinated collaboration across all roles throughout the project lifecycle. Understanding how roles interact during each phase helps ensure smooth handoffs, clear accountability, and efficient execution.

### Project Initiation
During initiation, the team defines the problem, validates the opportunity, and creates the project one-pager.
- **Product Managers** lead problem definition and success metrics
- **UX Designers** conduct initial user research and validate user needs
- **Data Analysts** provide baseline metrics and size the opportunity
- **Developers** assess technical feasibility and identify constraints
- **DevOps Engineers** evaluate infrastructure requirements and deployment considerations
- **Project Managers** facilitate kickoff, document stakeholders, and establish timelines
- **Customer Support Liaison** shares customer feedback and prioritizes pain points

### Project Planning
Planning transforms the approved initiative into a detailed execution plan with clear deliverables.
- **Product Managers** prioritize backlog items and define acceptance criteria
- **UX Designers** create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- **Data Analysts** define tracking requirements and experiment plans
- **Developers** estimate effort, identify dependencies, and propose technical design
- **DevOps Engineers** plan infrastructure changes and deployment strategy
- **Project Managers** create project timeline, risk register, and communication plan
- **Customer Support Liaison** prepares support documentation and escalation procedures

### Execution and Tracking
During execution, the team builds, tests, and iterates on the solution.
- **Developers** implement features, write tests, and participate in code reviews
- **UX Designers** refine designs based on implementation feedback and conduct usability tests
- **Data Analysts** validate tracking implementation and monitor early metrics
- **DevOps Engineers** maintain CI/CD pipelines and address infrastructure needs
- **Project Managers** track progress, manage risks, and coordinate cross-team dependencies
- **Product Managers** clarify requirements, make scope decisions, and prepare for launch
- **Customer Support Liaison** develops support materials and beta test coordination

### Release and Deployment
Release activities ensure the solution reaches users safely and successfully.
- **DevOps Engineers** execute deployment, monitor system health, and manage rollback if needed
- **Developers** support deployment, address critical bugs, and verify functionality in production
- **Data Analysts** monitor launch metrics and report on early adoption patterns
- **Product Managers** coordinate launch communications and validate success metrics
- **UX Designers** monitor user feedback and identify post-launch design improvements
- **Customer Support Liaison** handles launch communications, trains support team, and monitors customer feedback
- **Project Managers** document lessons learned and update stakeholders on launch status

### Retrospective and Continuous Improvement
Retrospectives capture learnings and identify opportunities for continuous improvement.
- **Project Managers** facilitate retrospective sessions and document action items
- **All team members** share what went well, what didn't, and improvement suggestions
- **Developers** propose process improvements for development workflows
- **DevOps Engineers** recommend infrastructure and deployment process enhancements
- **UX Designers** identify design process improvements and tooling needs
- **Data Analysts** share insights on measurement accuracy and reporting effectiveness
- **Product Managers** capture product insights and refine success metrics
- **Customer Support Liaison** provides customer impact assessment and support process feedback
